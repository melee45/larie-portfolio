About

A winding road
to one clear passion.

My path to software development wasn't straight. I started Computer Science in 2012, shifted to maritime studies, nearly completed a seafaring curriculum — then came back to CS because I knew I belonged in technology. That kind of conviction doesn't come from a straight line; it comes from lived experience.

Today I'm a consistent dean's lister at the University of Perpetual Help Biñan, finishing my CS degree (major in Data Science) this June 2025. Outside class I've been head of membership at an AWS Learning Club, volunteer staff at Generative AI events, and a regular at tech and startup events across the region.

I've built things that won competitions, things that failed, and things I'm still building. I love all of it — especially the parts where I had no idea what I was doing yet.
